--- layout: single author_profile: true --- I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Learning and Reasoning Group at the Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam. My research explores how graphs can be used as expressive representations of real-world phenomena, and how learning algorithms can exploit these representations to make predictions, answer queries, and support discovery. I work on both foundational aspects, such as neural architectures and algorithms for representation learning on graphs, and their applications to problems including relational reasoning, hypothesis generation over scientific knowledge graphs, and predictive models for science and engineering.
